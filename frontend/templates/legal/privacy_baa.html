BAA & Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 24, {{ current_year }}
Compliance Status: Meditab Portal is fully HIPAA-compliant and uses AES-256 encryption for all Protected Health Information (PHI).

1. HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA)

This Business Associate Agreement ("BAA") is entered into between Meditab Portal ("Business Associate") and the Healthcare Provider or Patient ("Covered Entity") utilizing our services.

Permitted Uses and Disclosures: Business Associate may use or disclose Protected Health Information (PHI) only as necessary to perform the services defined in the Terms of Service, specifically: transcribing patient audio, summarizing medical history, and facilitating doctor-patient communication.

Safeguards: Business Associate agrees to use appropriate safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of the PHI other than as provided for by this Agreement.

2. Data Collection Policy

We collect information necessary to provide medical management services. This includes:

  • Personal Identifiers: Name, Email, Phone Number, Date of Birth.
  • Medical Information: Symptoms, Medical History, Medication details provided via text or voice.

  • Uploaded Media: Medical reports (PDF/Images) and audio recordings. [cite: 5, 27]

3. Security of PHI

We implement technical and physical safeguards to protect your data:

  • Encryption at Rest: All database entries containing PHI are encrypted using AES-256 standards.
  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted between your device and our servers is protected via TLS 1.3.
  • Access Control: Strict Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) ensures only authorized providers can view patient data.

4. Voice & Upload Data Processing

When you use the "Tap to Speak" or File Upload features:

  • Audio is temporarily processed by our Speech-to-Text engine to generate a transcript.
  • Original audio files are stored securely in a dedicated HIPAA-compliant storage bucket with restricted access.
  • AI Summaries derived from this data are stored in your medical timeline for provider review.

5. Patient Rights

Under HIPAA, you have specific rights regarding your health data:

  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of your full medical timeline and chats at any time.
  • Right to Amendment: You may request corrections to your medical transcripts if errors are found.
  • Right to Accounting: You may request a log of who has accessed your medical records within our system.
